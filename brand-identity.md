## Values

### 1. Honesty
Radical transparency, delivered kindly. We show real results, real prices, real reviews. No filters on truth — only on photos when the user chooses.

### 2. Beauty
Look better to feel better. We believe aesthetic care is self-care, not vanity. Every design decision should make the user feel more confident, not more anxious.

### 3. Science
Evidence over hype. We cite sources, surface clinical data, and partner with board-certified providers. If the science isn't there, we say so.

### 4. Empathy
We build to connect, not to convince. Every interaction should feel like guidance from someone who genuinely cares about your skin health journey.

### 5. Inclusivity
Access for all skin tones. Our platform validates across the full Monk Skin Tone scale. Beauty is not one shade, one age, or one standard.

## Brand Voice

Vera speaks with **calm authority** — the passionate next-door Korean mom who knows every skin secret, every new clinic, every treatment worth your trust.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Intelligent** | Grounded in clinical evidence | "Retinol increases cell turnover by 25% — here's what that means for your skin." |
| **Warm** | Approachable, never cold | "We know the first consultation can feel overwhelming. We've been there too." |
| **Minimalist** | Says more with less | "3 treatments. 1 plan. Your best skin." |
| **Magnetic** | Draws you in with quiet confidence | "The women who age most beautifully? They started with a plan." |
| **Empowering** | Shows possibility, never prescribes | "Here's what's possible — the choice is always yours." |

### Tone Spectrum

```
Clinical ←————————●————→ Casual
         (slightly clinical)

Authoritative ←———●—————→ Friendly
               (balanced)

Serious ←—————————●——→ Playful
            (mostly serious)

Formal ←——————●—————→ Conversational
         (conversational-leaning)
```

### Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the benefit**, not the feature
2. **Use "you" and "your"** — never "users" or "customers"
3. **Avoid superlatives** — no "best," "amazing," "revolutionary"
4. **Cite when claiming** — link to sources for any medical/scientific claim
5. **Short sentences win** — if a sentence has a comma, consider splitting it
6. **Never shame** — no "anti-aging" fear tactics, no before/after pressure
7. **Korean terms are welcome** — but always translated on first use (e.g., "chok-chok (dewy, hydrated)")
